Info

David Bilek is a composer, performer, and concert organizer. He completed his B.Mus in Electroacoustic Composition at the Hochschule für Musik Franz Liszt Weimar in 2024 and is currently pursuing an M.Mus at the Institute of Sonology, Koninklijk Conservatorium Den Haag.

His works span contemporary and electroacoustic composition, installations, experimental live performances, and more club-oriented music and sound design. Exploring spaces from concert halls to underground clubs, David engages in different scenes and cultures, where he learns and adapts to new ways of listening and creating sound. It fascinates him how people listen and what they listen to. Connecting that with the question of what that says about their environment but also about themselves opens up a more empathic way of perceiving the world.

He has adapted a multitude of compositional perspectives, informed by sound ecologies, sound studies, field recording practice, new music, stochastic methods, minimal music, deep listening, radio, algorithmic music, multimedia, and many more. Composing for instruments and electronics opens up a world that spans transcultural ideas, delving deep into the past while also reflecting on our technological development. David prefers to tackle these topics with different people who bring not only musical but also visual, performative, or cultural backgrounds with them.

He engages with the European experimental music scene, playing concerts all over Europe and running the concert series Flüsterzimmer, which connects Weimar to the international music scene. He sees scene work as an essential part of artistry — working and organizing space together with the local community and allowing people from outside in, to inform and learn from each other.
